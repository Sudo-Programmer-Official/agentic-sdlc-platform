# Commercial Packaging and Pricing

## Purpose

This document defines how the **Agentic SDLC Platform** should be packaged and priced in the early commercial phase.

The goal is not to present final pricing as fixed truth. The goal is to create a practical commercial structure that can be used in:

- founder-led sales
- startup customer conversations
- design-partner offers
- accelerator applications
- early investor discussions

This pricing should be treated as an **initial commercial hypothesis** that will be refined through live customer conversations.

## Packaging Principles

The product should be sold around business outcomes, not technical internals.

Customers are not buying:

- a multi-agent system
- a prompt wrapper
- an experiment in autonomous coding

Customers are buying:

- faster software delivery with guardrails
- visible and reviewable AI execution
- reduced rework from uncontrolled changes
- a safer path to operational AI use in engineering

## Recommended Early Packages

### 1. Design Partner Pilot

This should be the primary commercial offer in the near term.

**Best for**

- technical founders
- startup CTOs
- small engineering teams
- agencies willing to trial a new workflow

**Duration**

- 4 to 8 weeks

**Scope**

- 1 to 3 bounded use cases
- onboarding into the workflow
- direct founder support
- feedback sessions
- success review at the end of the pilot

**Example use cases**

- scoped frontend feature delivery
- controlled backend change
- bounded test generation
- reviewable AI change proposal workflow

**What is included**

- environment setup
- repo onboarding
- workflow configuration
- live support during pilot
- weekly review
- end-of-pilot summary

**Indicative pricing hypothesis**

- `$8,000` to `$20,000` per pilot

The lower end is appropriate for very early design partners. The higher end is appropriate once the workflow is proving value.

### 2. Startup Team Plan

This is the first recurring SaaS-style offer.

**Best for**

- startups with 2 to 10 engineers
- product teams with frequent bounded changes
- technical founders using AI regularly but wanting stronger control

**Positioning**

- faster execution without blind repo drift
- structured runtime for small teams that do not have internal platform support

**Included capabilities**

- bounded runs
- execution visibility
- approval flow
- run history
- standard validation support
- basic architecture-aware guidance

**Indicative pricing hypothesis**

- `$1,000` to `$3,000` per month

The final structure can be:

- base platform fee
- included usage allowance
- overage or premium-run pricing

### 3. Growth Engineering Plan

This package is for teams that need stronger governance and operational consistency.

**Best for**

- startups scaling from 5 to 25 engineers
- product engineering teams standardizing AI usage
- agencies managing multiple software projects

**Positioning**

- team-level AI execution governance
- shared workflow instead of fragmented tool usage
- stronger review and policy discipline

**Included capabilities**

- team workspace governance
- richer run controls
- architecture profile support
- validation recipe customization
- usage analytics and policy support
- priority support

**Indicative pricing hypothesis**

- `$4,000` to `$10,000` per month

### 4. Enterprise Governance Program

This is not the first wedge, but it should be defined early for future conversations.

**Best for**

- platform engineering teams
- internal developer productivity groups
- organizations with approval, audit, and policy constraints

**Positioning**

- governed AI execution for serious engineering organizations
- safer AI adoption through policy, visibility, and review

**Included capabilities**

- advanced policy and approval controls
- organization-specific architecture and execution profiles
- expanded audit and reporting support
- onboarding and integration support
- security and governance review assistance

**Indicative pricing hypothesis**

- `$30,000` to `$120,000` annual contract value

This range should remain flexible until enterprise buying behavior is validated.

## Recommended Commercial Motion by Segment

### Startups

Lead with:

- speed with control
- reviewable output
- less AI-induced rework

Best package:

- Design Partner Pilot
- Startup Team Plan

### Agencies

- repeatable client delivery
- bounded execution
- reduced margin loss from rework

Best package:

- Design Partner Pilot
- Growth Engineering Plan

### Growing Product Teams

Lead with:

- standardizing AI-assisted engineering
- reducing fragmented workflows
- stronger governance across contributors

Best package:

- Growth Engineering Plan

### Enterprise or Platform Teams

Lead with:

- governance
- auditability
- architecture-aware controls

## Packaging by Buying Trigger

### Trigger: "We want AI to help, but we do not trust it yet."

Sell:

- Design Partner Pilot

### Trigger: "Our team already uses AI, but the workflow is messy."

Sell:

- Startup Team Plan
- Growth Engineering Plan

### Trigger: "We need policy and control before wider rollout."

Sell:

- Growth Engineering Plan
- Enterprise Governance Program

## What to Avoid

Do not price the product as:

- generic seat-based coding assistant software
- commodity inference access
- "unlimited autonomous engineer" magic

That pricing frame reduces the strategic value of the platform.

The product is not just another productivity tool. It is a control layer for AI software execution.

## Pricing Narrative

When discussing pricing, the message should be:

> We are not charging for raw code generation. We are charging for a governed software execution workflow that helps teams adopt AI with more trust, visibility, and discipline.

That is a stronger commercial story than "we help models write code."

## Early Commercial Terms Recommendation

For the first few customers:

- prefer pilot contracts over aggressive self-serve pricing
- keep onboarding high-touch
- tightly define scope
- capture measurable before-and-after feedback
- convert successful pilots into recurring subscriptions

## Suggested Success Metrics for Commercial Conversion

Use these metrics in pilots and renewals:

- number of successful bounded runs
- percentage of runs accepted after review
- reduction in manual rework
- time from request to reviewable output
- frequency of validation-backed completions
- number of safe repeatable workflows adopted by the team

## Final Recommendation

In the current phase, the best commercial posture is:

1. sell pilots first
2. validate one or two narrow use cases deeply
3. turn successful pilots into recurring startup or team plans
4. use enterprise conversations for discovery, not for early company dependence
