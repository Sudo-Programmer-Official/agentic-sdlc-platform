# Professor Email Templates

## Purpose

This document contains reusable email drafts for reaching out to a professor you have worked with earlier. The tone is respectful, professional, and slightly warm rather than overly formal.

## Template 1: First Outreach

**Subject options**

- Research Project Update and Request for Feedback
- Project Brief for Feedback: Agentic SDLC Platform
- Would Value Your Feedback on My Current Research-Oriented Project

**Email**

Dear Professor [Last Name],

I hope you are doing well. It has been some time since we worked together, and I wanted to share a project I have been building that I believe may have strong thesis and research potential.

The project is called **Agentic SDLC Platform**. Its main idea is to move beyond prompt-based coding assistants and instead build a runtime for AI-assisted software delivery that is:

- architecture-aware
- bounded by execution contracts
- validation-aware
- review-aware
- observable through a run console
- guided by reusable engineering memory

In short, the project tries to treat AI-assisted software engineering as a governed execution system rather than just a sequence of prompts.

I have prepared a short project brief and a one-page summary, and I would be very grateful for your feedback on:

- whether the research framing is strong
- whether the topic seems thesis-worthy or publication-worthy
- how you would recommend narrowing the scope or evaluating it

The research direction I currently find strongest is:

**Contract-Driven Autonomous Software Delivery: A Runtime Architecture for Safe, Architecture-Aware AI Software Execution**

If you are open to it, I would be happy to share the documents and, if possible, discuss the direction briefly at a time convenient for you.

Thank you for your time. I would really value your guidance.

Best regards,  
[Your Name]

## Suggested Attachments

For the first email, attach only:

- `PROFESSOR_ONE_PAGE_SUMMARY.md`
- `PROFESSOR_PROJECT_BRIEF.md`

If the professor shows interest, then send:

- `THESIS_PROPOSAL_FORMAL.md`
- `POTENTIAL_RESEARCH_TOPICS.md`
- `THESIS_PROJECT_DOCUMENT.md`

## Practical Advice

- Keep the first email short.
- Ask for feedback, not immediate supervision.
- Do not attach too many documents in the first message.
- Lead with the problem and novelty, not the length of implementation.
- Use the one-page summary first if the professor is busy.
